Vector and Matrix: Parametric Equations

Parametric equations change the plot command significantly. Each set of parametric equations is wrapped in square brackets, and inside each set of square brackets is three or four arguments, depending on 2D or 3D. Your first two arguments (or three, for 3D) are your x(t) and y(t) (and z(t)) and your third is a range for t.

restart
xt := t
yt := t^2
plot([xt,yt,t=-5..5])

Plotting two parametric equations doesn't change a whole lot. Let's do two spirals:

restart
xt := (4/5)*t*cos(t)
yt := t*sin(t)
xt2 := (2/5)*t*cos(t)
yt2 := (3/5)*t*sin(t)
plot({[xt, yt, t = 0 .. 20], [xt2, yt2, t = 0 .. 20]})

Arguments such as color, labels, title, etc all work just the same.
