What is Maple

Maple is a calculator. More specifically, Maple is a calculator on really strong steroids. The kind that make scrawny wimpy kids into huge football players. Or... your computer into the biggest and baddest calculator you've ever seen. Type one quick command and Maple will solve an integral. Type another command and it will solve a system of equations, or matrix multiplication. Another command solves any differential equation you can come up with. Maple makes your TI-84 look like your dad's sliderule (no offense to sliderules... they come in handy from time to time).

First Time Setup

  1. Open Maple. If there is a window with one of those check boxes that make it never show up again... I suggest clicking the check box.
  2. At the top menu, choose "Tools -> Options"
  3. Choose the third tab "Interface"
  4. Select the drop down menu with description: "Default format for new worksheets" and choose "Worksheet" instead of the default, "Document"
  5. At the bottom, click "Apply globally"
  6. Close Maple and reopen it
