Grade Sheet - Current Revision: 1.3.4

See the change log

Tired of your professor/teacher never updating the class website with your grades? I sure am. I've created a spreadsheet that is very easy and quick to use, which will help you keep track of your grades, without depending on your prof to update Angel or whatever website they (don't) use.

Microsoft Office Excel
OpenOffice Calc

I tried to use column headings and labels that are self-explanatory, but if you need help using the form, feel free to either ask me or read the instructions below.
Note: Older file-types available upon request.

Instructions:

  1. Setup:
    • Find the course syllabus for each of your classes. The syllabus lists the weight of each type of assignment (Ex. Homework: 20%, Tests: 40%, Final: A lot%)
    • Open the grade sheet and go to File -> Save (or Control + S) and rename the file with the name of one of your courses. Continue doing a Save As for each of your courses, so that you have one file for each course.
  2. Fill in each of the boxes labeled "Weight" with the percent listed in your syllabi. Check for typos by looking at the weight of the final - it is automatically calculated.
    By now, you may have noticed that not all cells can be selected. This is to keep you from accidentally editing a formula. There is no password on the protection, so if you know what you're doing, feel free to edit whatever you need. Please let me know, as I'd love to find some way to incorporate your changes into my next revision.
  3. If you have special items such as attendance or projects, the Miscellaneous section is a good place to put them. Enter in the weight of each misc. item and under "Grade (%)" enter in the percentage grade you received, not the number of points.
  4. The bolded cell titled "Grade before final" takes into account all grades (including those in Misc.) except for your final. This now works all of the time. Thanks to some hidden formulas in the document, sections of the class that have not yet been graded, such as projects or attendance, will not be taken into account.
  5. The subsections of "Grade before final" are rather useless to be honest. They tell you how many percentage points each section of your work gives to your total grade (including final). For instance, if your homework average is an 80%, and it is worth 20% of your overall grade, "From Homework" will read 16%.
  6. Note: The next two sections will have the greatest accuracy only when all sections of your grade contain something. If a project has yet to be completed, try entering what you expect to receive for that project.
  7. In "Desired Grade", enter the grade you would like to receive in the class. The following cell tells you, with the current points you've received throughout the class, what grade you must get on your final to achieve your goal. If it's bolded and red, you're screwed.
  8. Further down is a cell, "Expected grade". Here you may enter what you expect to get on the final, and the following cell tells you what grade this will result in. If it is bolded and red, this means your expected grade is not high enough to achieve your desired grade. Study up!
